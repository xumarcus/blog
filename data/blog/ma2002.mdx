# Addendum
There is an interesting problem from one of his lectures:
Let $P: \mathbf{Q} \to \mathbf{Q}$ satisfies $P_{k}(n) = \sum_{j=1}^{n} j^{k}$ whenever $n \in \mathbf{N}$. Prove that $P_{2k}(\frac{1}{2}) = \frac{1}{4^{k}}$.
